US Candy Distribution: Sales & Profitability Performance Analysis

Objective: 
The primary objective of this project is to analyze the sales performance, product contribution, regional distribution, and profitability of the US candy distribution business.

This analysis aims to:
Evaluate overall financial performance (Sales, Cost, Profit)
Identify top- and low-performing products
Analyze regional sales contribution
Understand unit distribution by product division
Provide actionable insights to improve profitability and operational efficiency
Support strategic decision-making with data-driven recommendations

Insight:
The company maintains a strong gross profit margin, indicating effective pricing or cost management.

Data Source: 
https://mavenanalytics.io/data-playground/us-candy-distributor

Domain: Sales

Problem Statement:
What is the total revenue, cost, and gross profit?
Which products generate the highest sales?
Which products underperform and may require strategic review?
How is revenue distributed across regions?
Which divisions contribute the most units sold?
Where are opportunities for growth and optimization?

Data Preprocessing Steps: 

Data Collection:
Gathered data from MavenAnalytics(https://mavenanalytics.io/)

Data Cleaning (Power Query):
Standardized text formats, corrected data types.

Data Transformation:
Added calculated columns, tables and Measures.

Data Integration:
Appended cleaned datasets into a unified dataset for visualization.

Analysis & Visualizations:

Developed interactive Power BI dashboards addressing all problem statements using:
Clustered Bar Charts
Pie Chart
Stacked Area Chart
Tree Maps
Matrix Table
Azure Maps

Highlights: 

The top five Wonka Bars contribute the majority of total sales.
Core chocolate bar variants drive overall business performance.
High brand consistency indicates strong product-market fit. 
Several SKUs contribute negligible revenue.
  Poor demand
  Limited distribution
  Inefficient marketing
  Inventory inefficiencies
Regional demand is moderately balanced but skewed toward Pacific and Atlantic.
Strong margin indicates:
  Effective pricing strategy
  Controlled production cost
  High contribution from premium products

Key Findings:

Chocolate-based Wonka Bars drive the majority of revenue.
Pacific and Atlantic regions lead in performance.
Several products generate negligible sales.
Strong gross margin (~66%) shows healthy financial structure.
Revenue concentration risk exists in a few top SKUs.

Recommendations:

1️ Product Optimization:
Phase out or re-evaluate low-performing SKUs.
Bundle slow movers with top-selling products.

2️ Regional Strategy:
Replicate Pacific region marketing strategy in Gulf.
Evaluate regional pricing sensitivity.

3️ Diversification Strategy:
Invest in developing Sugar and Other divisions.
Introducing new Flavors or seasonal SKUs.

4️ Cost & Margin Analysis:
Conduct a detailed SKU-level profitability study.
Identify cost-saving opportunities in logistics and manufacturing.

5️ Data Enhancement:
Add time-series analysis (monthly trends).
Forecast demand using predictive analytics models.

Conclusion:
The US Candy Distribution business demonstrates strong financial health driven primarily by chocolate-based Wonka products. However, revenue concentration risk and underperforming SKUs present optimization opportunities.
Strategic regional expansion, SKU rationalization, and portfolio diversification can significantly enhance long-term growth and operational efficiency.
